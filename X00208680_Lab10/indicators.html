Indicators for UN SDG 11 - Sustainable Cities and Communities

Sustainable Development Goal 11 has several indicators to measure progress towards creating inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable cities and human settlements.

Key Indicators:

  1. Proportion of urban population living in slums, informal settlements, or inadequate housing.
  2. Proportion of population with convenient access to public transport, by sex, age, and persons with disabilities.
  3. Proportion of cities with a direct participation structure of civil society in urban planning and management that operate regularly and democratically.
  4. Proportion of financial support to the least developed countries for sustainable and resilient buildings.
  5. Number of deaths, missing persons, and directly affected persons attributed to disasters per 100,000 population.
  6. Proportion of local governments that adopt and implement local disaster risk reduction strategies in line with the Sendai Framework for Disaster Risk Reduction 2015-2030.
  7. Proportion of the population that has convenient access to public transport, by age, sex, and persons with disabilities.
  8. Proportion of urban solid waste regularly collected and with adequate final discharge out of total urban solid waste generated, by cities.
  9. Annual mean levels of fine particulate matter (e.g., PM2.5 and PM10) in cities.
  10. Average share of the built-up area of cities that is open space for public use for all, by sex, age, and persons with disabilities.
